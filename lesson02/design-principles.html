Design Principle Examples

Hick's Law

"The time it takes to make a decision increases with the number and complexity of choices." In other words, less complexity puts less pressure on the user to make a choice, and they will make a choice in less time. Here, Google makes it easier to make a decision by reducing visual clutter and influences.

Fitt's Law

"The time to acquire a target is a function of the distance to and size of the target." Making a touchable target or element on your mobile webpage also requires you to figure its size. If your button elements are too small, it frustrates the user and makes them take more time trying to tap what they want. On Microsoft's website, they made their nav buttons nice and large for ease of tapping them.

Visual Hierarchy

Different elements, depending on their characteristics, may stand out more than others. With this in mind, there can be an implied order or importance to elements of a webpage. Giving elements emphasis can help users know what they should look at to find information. There are other things to keep in mind, like keeping your colors similar for sake of unity, or using different colors to create a "pop".
